Frequently Asked Questions

Q. I entered a list of genes into Human SSA and I noticed that several were excluded from the analysis. Why is this?

A. This can happen for several reasons. The background data set was built by extracting all human/mouse ortholog pairs from Ensembl, determining the promoter region, aligning the sequences and scanning the conserved portions within the promoter regions for putative TFBSs. In many cases, strict one-to-one human/mouse orthologs were not defined. Out of the orthologs which were defined, some did not align in such a way that the conserved promoter region could be identified in a satisfactory way. These genes were therefore excluded from the oPOSSUM background set. Additionally, not all genes in the set could be mapped in all other gene identification schemas. In the latter case you could try entering your list of genes using a different gene identification system, preferably Ensembl Gene ID.
